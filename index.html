    The Origins of Nations on a Global Scale
  •  
  • Whenever we start to think about the origin of a certain system, we will involuntarily bring up geographical determinism, cultural determinism, and even racial determinism, and believe that a certain political system and political habit must be influenced by external forces or due to certain traditional habits during the historical development process. Under this influence, a system with significant characteristics is extracted from the entire nation or political body through long-term evolution. From the perspective of future generations, this extracted system can be used as a summary of a certain national historical stage, and can even be used as a summary of the national character and its historical origins. Undoubtedly, it is extremely important and worthy of in-depth study. The study of the origins of systems can not only enhance our understanding of the history of a certain nation or country after its system has been fully constructed, but also allow us to understand the origins of its nationality and national character, and to understand the different trends of national history in different geographical and cultural environments. It can also be used to explain how the inherent contradictions between various ethnic groups and countries in today's world arise, and we can use this to think about how to bridge the contradictions between different ethnic groups that have taken different paths thousands of years ago, in order to achieve what Kant called "permanent peace".Read More...

    Debate on the Democratic Transformation of Modern China
  •  
  • Modern democracy, or the democratic theory we are discussing, is not in line with the classical democracy of the Athenian city-state, nor is it homogeneous with the ideas produced under various declarations and movements. Therefore, we always have some misunderstandings about the term democracy and the actual essence of this ancient system, which is the spirit of democracy. If the term democracy is placed under ideological rhetoric, then this concept often departs from actual institutional arrangements and is simply used as a means of propaganda to flaunt and conceal one's weaknesses in institutional arrangements, or simply exists as a rhetoric. If democracy is placed under what we call Western rhetoric, we will always subconsciously associate it with parliamentary systems, electoral systems, and elite politics. This phenomenon is not puzzling, as in reality, the democratic system of modern Western countries is a system constructed with parliamentary and electoral systems as its core. This system actually emphasizes a kind of political "procedural justice", whose essence is to legitimize the exercise of public power, preserve the existence of the system, prevent the system from being destroyed by speculators and ambitious individuals, eliminate the natural contradictions between various interest groups and people with different political tendencies in society to the greatest extent possible, and transfer the game to the political stage. In the theory of social contract, there is a saying that each of us, together with all our forces, is placed under the highest guidance of public will, and we accept each member as an inseparable part of the whole in the community. Therefore, whether viewed from the democratic system of ancient Greece or the modern electoral system, the commonality that exists in different systems is the paradigm of democracy, which is what Rousseau meant by the guidance of public will, that is, the generation and exercise of public power. Whether this process is legal and recognized by the participants of democratic politics is a single indicator for judging whether a political entity is democratic. Correspondingly, this paradigm reflects the spirit of democracy, and the continuous development of democratic theory aims to achieve true legalization of public power through various systems under different frameworks. The goal of constructing a democratic system is to achieve the pursuit of liberalism under a gradually improving system.Read More...
